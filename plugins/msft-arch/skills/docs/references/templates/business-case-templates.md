# Business Case Templates

## Overview

Business cases justify technology investments by quantifying costs, benefits, and risks. This guide provides templates for creating compelling financial analyses that secure executive approval for Microsoft ecosystem initiatives.

**Skills-First Reminder**: Before creating ANY business case, ALWAYS check for available skills:
- **xlsx skill** → Financial models, ROI calculations, TCO analysis, benefit tracking, sensitivity analysis
- **docx skill** → Business case narrative, executive summaries, supporting documentation
- **pptx skill** → Executive presentations, investment committee briefings
- **pdf skill** → Final business case packages for approval and distribution

## Comprehensive Business Case Template

### When to Use
Use the comprehensive business case template for major transformation initiatives requiring executive or board approval. Essential for:
- Cloud migration programs (>$500K investment)
- Digital transformation initiatives
- Enterprise platform implementations
- Multi-year strategic programs

## Best Practices for Business Cases

### 1. Skills-First Approach
- ALWAYS use **xlsx skill** for financial models (calculations, scenarios, charts)
- Use **docx skill** for narrative and supporting documentation
- Use **pptx skill** for executive presentations
- Use **pdf skill** for final approval packages

### 2. Be Conservative with Benefits
- Use validated data from pilots or industry benchmarks
- Discount optimistic estimates by 20-30%
- Ramp benefits gradually (avoid "hockey stick" projections)
- Include only benefits you can measure and attribute

### 3. Be Realistic with Costs
- Add 15-20% contingency for unknowns
- Include often-forgotten costs (training, change management, ongoing support)
- Factor in internal resource costs, not just external
- Plan for cost overruns in sensitivity analysis

### 4. Validate Through Pilots
- Use Validate phase to prove benefits before full investment
- Update business case with actual pilot results
- Adjust projections based on real data

### 5. Make It Measurable
- Define KPIs upfront
- Establish baseline metrics
- Plan quarterly benefits tracking
- Assign ownership for benefit realization

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Overly optimistic benefits**: Use conservative estimates, validate assumptions
2. **Underestimating costs**: Include ALL costs (people, training, change, ongoing)
3. **Ignoring qualitative benefits**: Not everything is quantifiable, but still valuable
4. **No sensitivity analysis**: Always show best/worst/likely scenarios
5. **Forgetting skills check**: Use **xlsx skill** for all financial models
6. **Static business case**: Update quarterly with actuals vs. projections
7. **No benefit tracking**: Plan how you'll measure and report on benefits
8. **Missing stakeholder buy-in**: Involve finance and business leaders early
