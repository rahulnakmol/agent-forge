> **Architect Notes**: The Role Based Process Flows are organized in the Process taxonomy format. This is the Procurement Process taxonomy that covers Source-to-Contract, Requisition-to-Pay >and Process Governance. The process taxonomy depicts end-to-end key processes of an entire function on a single page. This establishes a common nomenclature and numbering convention for the other components of the Target Operating Model. The Procurement taxonomy shows the group of end-to-end processes for the Procurement business operations, which includes activities involving requisitioning, purchase order creation, and invoice verification. The processes are group by levels 1, 2 and 3. Below each level 1 process header is a list of subcategories we call level 2 processes and below each level 2 are the Level 3 sub-processes organized by the achievement of an outcome. From here, let's drill down to Level 4. These customized taxonomies will standardize how our teams talk about processes across your organization.

## Review & Approve Requisition process flow in ARIS

> **Architect Notes**: When you drill down to the next layer, Layer 4, we get to the actual process flows showing which tasks are performed by whom and in what order. Most of our TOM assets are housed in ARIS, which is a robust enterprise modeling tool and a key component of our Powered execution suite. This is the process flow for reviewing and approving requisitions. Each of the yellow boxes to the left is a functional role and the tasks they perform are represented by green boxes on the right. These swim lanes make it easy to see who is responsible for each task. Tasks and processes are indexed so that you can easily see where they fit in the overall taxonomy. They are also linked by gateway markers that indicate if a task is performed in parallel or in sequence. Next let's explore the tasks. The task boxes have a lot of information built in. Let’s zoom into a couple tasks to take a closer look.

## Role Based Process Flows – Tasks

> **Architect Notes**: The red CP at the top right indicates that this has a control point. The red O in the bottom left means that this activity is performed manually in the software system. A blue “A” would indicate that the activity is automated, and a red M would indicate that the task is performed outside of the system manually. The teal R you see on the top left indicates that there is a report associated with this activity.
