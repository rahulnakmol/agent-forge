# Discovery Questioning Framework

Comprehensive question bank for progressive discovery. Questions are organized by the five discovery dimensions and grouped in batches of 2-3 for use with `AskUserQuestion`. Each batch is tagged with the relevant entry mode.

## Questioning Principles

1. **Batch size**: Always ask 2-3 questions per interaction. Never bombard with more.
2. **Progressive depth**: Start broad, narrow based on responses.
3. **Mode awareness**: Some questions only apply to SaaS (A) or Consulting (B) contexts.
4. **Adaptive sequencing**: Skip questions already answered. Probe deeper where responses are vague.
5. **Open-ended first**: Lead with open questions, follow up with specific ones.

---

## Dimension 1: Business Context

### Batch 1.1 -- Initial Context (Both Modes)
- What is the core business problem or opportunity you are trying to address?
- What industry and market segment does this organization operate in?

### Batch 1.2 -- Strategic Alignment (Both Modes)
- What are the top 2-3 strategic objectives this initiative supports?
- Is there an existing strategic roadmap or transformation plan this fits into?

### Batch 1.3 -- Business Model (Mode A: SaaS)
- How does the product generate revenue (subscription tiers, usage-based, freemium, transactional)?
- What is the current stage of the product (pre-launch, growth, maturity, renewal)?
- Who is the buyer vs the end user (are they the same person)?

### Batch 1.4 -- Organizational Context (Mode B: Consulting)
- What is the organizational structure (centralized, federated, matrix)?
- How many business units, regions, or legal entities are affected?
- Is there an existing transformation design or SOW from a prior consulting engagement?

### Batch 1.5 -- Competitive Landscape (Mode A: SaaS)
- Who are the top 2-3 competitors or alternative solutions?
- What is the primary differentiator for this product?

### Batch 1.6 -- Regulatory Context (Both Modes)
- Are there regulatory, compliance, or legal requirements that constrain the solution?
- Are there industry-specific standards that must be followed (SOX, HIPAA, GDPR, etc.)?

## Dimension 2: Stakeholder Landscape

### Batch 2.1 -- Key Players (Both Modes)
- Who is the executive sponsor or ultimate decision-maker for this initiative?
- Who are the primary stakeholders that will be involved day-to-day?

### Batch 2.2 -- User Identification (Mode A: SaaS)
- Who are the primary user personas (describe their roles, not their names)?
- How do these users currently solve the problem today (workarounds, competitor tools, manual processes)?
- What frustrations or pain points do they express most frequently?

### Batch 2.3 -- Organizational Actors (Mode B: Consulting)
- Who are the process owners for the affected business functions?
- Which roles currently execute the processes under consideration?
- Are there external parties (vendors, partners, regulators) involved in these processes?

### Batch 2.4 -- Influence Mapping (Both Modes)
- Who can block or accelerate this initiative (formal or informal influence)?
- Are there any known resistors to change or competing priorities?

### Batch 2.5 -- Team Structure (Both Modes)
- What team will be responsible for executing this initiative?
- Are there skill gaps that need to be addressed (training, hiring, contracting)?

---

## Dimension 3: Problem Definition

### Batch 3.1 -- Problem Statement (Both Modes)
- In one sentence, what is the problem this initiative solves?
- What is the measurable impact of this problem today (cost, time, errors, churn, revenue)?

### Batch 3.2 -- Root Cause Exploration (Both Modes)
- Why does this problem exist? What is the underlying root cause?
- Has this problem been addressed before? If so, what was tried and why did it not work?

### Batch 3.3 -- User Pain Points (Mode A: SaaS)
- What are the top 3 pain points users experience in the current workflow?
- At what point in the user journey do users drop off, complain, or seek alternatives?
- What do users say they wish the product could do?

### Batch 3.4 -- Process Gaps (Mode B: Consulting)
- Which existing processes are the biggest bottlenecks or sources of error?
- Where do manual handoffs or rework occur most frequently?
- Are there process steps that exist only because of system limitations?

### Batch 3.5 -- Scope Boundaries (Both Modes)
- What is explicitly in scope for this initiative?
- What is explicitly out of scope (even if related)?

---

## Dimension 4: Constraints & Dependencies

### Batch 4.1 -- Budget and Timeline (Both Modes)
- What is the budget range for this initiative (fixed, flexible, or TBD)?
- Are there hard deadlines (contractual, regulatory, market-driven)?

### Batch 4.2 -- Technical Constraints (Both Modes)
- What existing systems, platforms, or tools must be integrated with or preserved?
- Are there technology mandates or preferences (cloud platform, language, vendor)?

### Batch 4.3 -- Organizational Constraints (Both Modes)
- What is the organization's capacity for change right now (other initiatives in flight)?
- Are there political or cultural factors that could affect adoption?

### Batch 4.4 -- Dependencies (Both Modes)
- What other initiatives, teams, or vendors does this initiative depend on?
- Is there data that needs to be available before this initiative can proceed?

---

## Dimension 5: Success Criteria

### Batch 5.1 -- Measurable Outcomes (Both Modes)
- What are the 2-3 key metrics that will define success?
- What are the current baselines for these metrics?

### Batch 5.2 -- User Success (Mode A: SaaS)
- How will you measure user adoption and satisfaction?
- What does a successful user journey look like after this initiative?

### Batch 5.3 -- Process Success (Mode B: Consulting)
- What process KPIs will improve (cycle time, error rate, cost per transaction)?
- How will you measure organizational adoption of new processes?

### Batch 5.4 -- Acceptance Criteria (Both Modes)
- What must be true for the executive sponsor to consider this initiative complete?
- Are there intermediate milestones where progress should be demonstrated?

## Adaptive Follow-Up Questions

Use these when initial responses are vague or incomplete.

### Clarifying Vague Problem Statements
- "You mentioned [X] -- can you give me a specific example of when this happened?"
- "When you say [X] is a problem, what is the impact in terms of time, cost, or customer experience?"

### Uncovering Hidden Stakeholders
- "Who else would need to be consulted before a decision is made on this?"
- "Are there downstream teams that would be affected by changes in this area?"

### Probing Constraints
- "You mentioned a tight timeline -- is that driven by a specific event or is it aspirational?"
- "When you say budget is flexible, does that mean there is executive support for investment if the business case is strong?"

### Deepening Root Cause
- "Why does [stated cause] happen? What causes that?"
- "If [stated cause] were resolved, would the problem go away entirely or would something else remain?"
