> **Architect Notes**: The process taxonomy depicts end-to-end key processes of an entire function on a single page. This establishes a common nomenclature and numbering convention for the other components of the Target Operating Model. This taxonomy shows the key categories of the Cyber function in dark blue. We call these Level 1 processes. Cyber has 6 of these level 1 processes. Below each level 1 process header is a list of subcategories we call level 2 processes. Each level 1 process has a separate taxonomy outlining level 2 and level 3 processes.

> **Architect Notes**: For example, here we can see the taxonomy for the 1st level 1 process, Identity Governance. This taxonomy has 10 level 2 processes shown in dark blue. The level 3 processes are shown below in light blue. If we drill down to the next layer, we get to the actual process flows showing which tasks are performed by whom and in what order. Process taxonomies serve as a framework for the end-to-end processes in each function. When we kick off your transformation, we will use these to help define the scope of the engagement. We will add missing processes and remove irrelevant processes to customize these taxonomies to your organization. We will continue to use this as a heat map to capture your pain points. These customized taxonomies will standardize how our teams talk about processes across your organization.
