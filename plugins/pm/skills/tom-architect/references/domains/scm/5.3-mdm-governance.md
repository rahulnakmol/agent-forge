> **Architect Notes**: The Master Data Management, or MDM, Design and Governance asset is the third of three assets in the Performance Insights & Data layer of the Target Operating Model. It provides a structural guide of how master data is designed and governed, enabling you to manage and govern master data during and after a Powered implementation. The MDM asset augments and aggregates other components of the Target Operating Model including the Process, People, Technology, Performance Insights & Data, and Governance design layers to explain what drives the way master data looks and how it is structured. As you can see here, while the Master Data Management template resides under Performance Insights & Data, it impacts several other areas, including Process, Technology, and Governance. The Tech Agnostic MDM asset addresses the business requirements and functional solutions for key master data domains. The Platform Specific MDM assets capture the technical solutions required to address the business requirements for key master data domains.

## MDM Definition & Design Principles

| Master Data Domain Name: | Customer Master |
| --- | --- |
| Primary Use of this Master Data: | Aggregate customer data from multiple sources, enrich data and maintain an accurate single source of truth for the customer. The results lead to improved customer experience and enhance reporting that provides insights into sales and profitability. |

| Design Principles Guiding Structure of this Master Data (Both General and Platform Specific): | 1. Create a centralized single version of truth around customer data2. Customer master data is controlled by a centralized team and all changes to the master data should be subject to business approval3. Common/standard data model and data structure requirements so that customer, supplier and product data is stored consistently in all applications across the organization 4. Periodic reviews should be performed on the customer master file to maintain the integrity of customer information5. Customer records conform to a consistent naming convention that is applied across the organization6. Governance processes are established to manage shared customer data across multiple applications 7. Leverage hierarchy management to link and relate customer names, addresses, locations to better target customers and build loyalty8. Establish tracking mechanism to identify the source system providing the customer master update9. Establish self-service capability for customers to maintain their information |  |  |  |  |  |  |
