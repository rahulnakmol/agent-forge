The letter ‘F’ on the right represents alignment to the Future of Sustainability. In other words, this is where the cutting-edge industry leaders are rated.

It’s important to remember that an organization cannot usually jump more than 2 levels in a given transformation. Progress takes time. Also, achieving a 5 rating in every category is not necessarily advisable. It takes a great amount of focus and resources to get the highest rating. Unless being the industry leader in a given category is key to your company’s strategy, you may want to allocate your time and budget elsewhere.

## What’s the gap? How do we close it?

The business is not doing well in handling the complexity of the business using the current technology, and further functionality could be leveraged to bring baseline benefits.
ESG will challenge and change ways of working, and therefore it is not looking toward a Microsoft implementation.
Getting staff trained on ESG has been a challenge in the past.
As-Is
Relative departments will work collaboratively with Business Units to determine the right technology that best supports the end-to-end processes for Sustainability
ESG teams will have a sound understanding of the end-to-end process with technology having the right functionality creating an efficient, customer focused, and agile function.
Opportunities to maximize technology by expanding functionalities will be identified.
To-Be
Right technology with the right functionalities, supporting the end-to-end processes, creating a Sustainability function that is efficient, customer-focused and agile.
