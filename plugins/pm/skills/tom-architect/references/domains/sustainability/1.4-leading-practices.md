| Level 3 Name | Leading Practice / Design Consideration Name | Description (further detail) | Description of Benefits |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identify Scope of Reporting | Determine level of detail for reporting on entities in scope | Consider the level of detail needed for data related to legal entities/subsidiaries, portfolio companies, departments or organizational units, physical locations, and physical assets. | Collecting more detailed data (such as emissions for specific organization units or physical assets) enables detailed analysis of sustainability issues and enables more data validation. |
| Identify Applicable ESG Reporting Regulations | Identify and monitor regulatory reporting requirements | Research ESG reporting requirements in all jurisdictions where the organization operates. Governments (e.g. EU) and financial authorities (e.g. SEC) may set regulations about mandatory ESG reporting or may recommend non-mandatory reporting. Check for updated requirements before each reporting cycle. | The organization can comply to reporting regulations, thus avoiding penalties. The organization can follow recommended reporting practices, which may benefit relations with various stakeholders. |
| Identify Applicable ESG Reporting Regulations | Identify pertinent details of ESG regulations | Identify relevant details for ESG reporting regulations including regulation status, scope/applicability, effective date, reporting frequency, and assurance requirements. It is important to consider if the regulation is mandatory or voluntary, which companies or industries the regulation applies to, which specific ESG factors are being evaluated, and when the reporting obligations begin. | Identifying pertinent details will help companies comply with mandatory ESG reporting requirements and meet stakeholder expectations for voluntary ESG reporting. By understanding the regulation status, scope/applicability, effective date, reporting frequency, and assurance requirements, a company can develop a robust ESG reporting framework that covers the necessary areas and meets the reporting frequency requirements. |

> **Architect Notes**: Design considerations are significant decisions a client makes when designing a platform solution. This Leading Practices asset provides a specific point of view on how something should be designed and what benefits result from that design decision. It shows where in the process the impact is felt and in which software module the design should be considered.
